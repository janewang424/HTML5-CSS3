Plane wave (PW) imaging provides high frame rate (> 10 kHz) that makes it a good technique for tracking shear waves; however, the unfocused transmission beam creates high side-lobe levels. In this paper, we will demonstrate that distributing more sub-apertures over multiple pushdetect events overcomes the frame rate induced limitation; we will also show that distributing plane wave compounding over numerous push-detect events allows more compounding which also improves tracking.
